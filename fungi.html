Fungi

Fungi are multicellular or unicellular eukaryotic organisms that absorb nutrients from their environment. They play a vital role as decomposers in ecosystems.

Fungi include yeasts, molds, and mushrooms. They have cell walls made of chitin and do not perform photosynthesis. Instead, they release enzymes to break down organic material and absorb nutrients. Fungi can form symbiotic relationships with plants (mycorrhizae) or live as parasites.

Fungi are essential for nutrient cycling, decomposing dead organisms, and maintaining soil health. They are also important in food production, medicine (antibiotics), and biotechnology. Studying fungi helps us understand ecology, evolution, and practical applications.
