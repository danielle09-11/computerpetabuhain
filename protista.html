Protista

Protista is a diverse kingdom consisting mostly of unicellular eukaryotic organisms. They can be plant-like, animal-like, or fungus-like, and live mainly in moist environments.

Protists are mostly single-celled eukaryotes that have a true nucleus and membrane-bound organelles. Some protists are autotrophic and perform photosynthesis, like algae, while others are heterotrophic, like amoebas and paramecia. There are also slime molds and water molds, which behave similarly to fungi.

Protists are important for ecological balance as they serve as primary producers, decomposers, and a food source for other organisms. Studying protists helps scientists understand evolution, cell biology, and the origin of multicellular life.
