Animalia

Animalia is the kingdom of multicellular, eukaryotic organisms that are heterotrophic, meaning they consume other organisms for energy. They are highly diverse in form, habitat, and behavior.

Animals are multicellular eukaryotes with complex organ systems. They lack cell walls and can move voluntarily. This kingdom includes invertebrates like insects and mollusks, as well as vertebrates like fish, birds, mammals, and reptiles. Animals have specialized structures for sensing their environment, obtaining food, and reproducing.

Studying animals helps us understand behavior, physiology, evolution, and ecology. Animals play critical roles in ecosystems as predators, prey, pollinators, and seed dispersers. Protecting animal diversity is essential for maintaining ecological balance and biodiversity.
